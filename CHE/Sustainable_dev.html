ABOUT

Sustainable food waste management is a systematic approach to reducing food waste and its associated impacts. It involves managing food and agricultural products for better purposes, such as: Human consumption, Animal feed, Industrial application, Environmental benefits.

Sustainable management of food is important because it helps to conserve resources, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and provide access to food for those who need it. Wasted food is a growing problem, with about 66 million tons of wasted food generated in the U.S. in 2019 alone. This wasted food contributes to climate change and takes up valuable landfill space. Additionally, it is estimated that one-third of all food produced for human consumption is lost or wasted. This represents a significant loss of resources and a missed opportunity to nourish people. Taking simple steps to reduce food waste can make a big difference in addressing this issue.

Food Waste Management for Sustainable Development Goals

Food waste management plays a crucial role in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) and SDG 12 (Ensure Sustainable Consumption and Production Patterns). By reducing food waste, we can contribute to food security, alleviate poverty, conserve resources, and mitigate climate change.

Tackling Food Waste for SDG 2: Zero Hunger

Food waste is a paradox in a world where millions suffer from hunger. Approximately one-third of all food produced globally is lost or wasted, amounting to an estimated 1.3 billion tons of food annually. This wasted food could have nourished over 2 billion people. Reducing food waste is essential to ensure that everyone has access to enough nutritious food to meet their dietary needs.

Optimizing Food Systems for SDG 12: Sustainable Consumption and Production

Food waste is a major contributor to environmental degradation and resource depletion. The production, processing, and transportation of food require significant resources, including water, energy, and land. When food is wasted, these resources are wasted as well. Additionally, food waste contributes to greenhouse gas emissions, exacerbating climate change.

Strategies for Effective Food Waste Management

Addressing food waste requires a comprehensive approach that encompasses all stages of the food supply chain, from production to consumption. Here are some key strategies :

  • Improving post-harvest handling and storage: Losses during harvesting, storage, and transportation are significant contributors to food waste. Implementing better handling practices, using appropriate storage technologies, and reducing transportation distances can minimize these
  • Enhancing food distribution and marketing: Inefficient distribution systems and inaccurate demand forecasting lead to food surpluses that often end up being wasted. Improving distribution channels, strengthening market linkages, and reducing food loss between producers and consumers can help address these issues.
  • Educating consumers and businesses: Raising awareness about food waste and its consequences among consumers and businesses can encourage behavioral changes that lead to waste reduction. This includes promoting proper food storage, encouraging date labeling practices, and reducing portion sizes.
  • Developing innovative technologies and solutions: Technological advancements can play a significant role in reducing food waste. Innovations such as smart packaging, intelligent food monitoring systems, and food waste upcycling technologies can contribute to more efficient food management and resource recovery.
By implementing these strategies and fostering a culture of sustainable food consumption, we can make significant progress in reducing food waste and achieving the SDGs.

International Day of Awareness of Food Loss and Waste
The UN General Assembly designated September 29 as the International Day of Awareness of Food Loss and Waste in 2019. The 2023 theme was "Reducing food loss and waste: Taking Action to Transform Food Systems".

Day of Zero Waste
The 2023 theme was "Achieving sustainable and environmentally sound practices of minimizing and managing waste". The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs organized a rally for garbage free cities in New Delhi.

Global Food Loss and Waste Pledge
The Food is Never Waste Coalition developed this pledge ahead of the 2022 United Nations Climate Change Conference. The pledge called on governments, businesses, and institutions to make voluntary commitments.
